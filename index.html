About Me

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Bioinformatics at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen School of Medicine, expected to graduate in 2027. My research interests focus on single-cell omics, multi-omics data integration, and computational biology.

I am particularly familiar with single-cell RNA-seq data analysis pipelines, including quality control, clustering, cell type annotation, differential expression analysis, and downstream biological interpretation. I am also interested in applying machine learning and AI-based approaches to biomedical research and precision medicine.

In Summer 2025, I worked as a visiting student at the SMART Shenzhen Institutes of Advanced Technology, where I analyzed single-cell transcriptomic data of intracardiac neurons and contributed to a mouse sleep-related project. In Spring 2026, I joined University of California, Berkeley as an exchange student and Undergraduate Visiting Researcher in Prof. Peng He's laboratory at the University of California, San Francisco, focusing on identifying rare cell types in human kidney tumors through integrated cell atlases. I later interned at Merck Serono as a Medical Affairs Intern, supporting clinical medicine with scientific and biomedical research insights. These experiences further strengthened my interest in computational biology and translational biomedical research.

I am passionate about applying computational approaches to biomedical research, particularly in single-cell and multi-omics data analysis. I am also interested in the potential of AI-driven methods in biology and medicine, and I hope to contribute to the next generation of computational tools that bridge biology, medicine, and artificial intelligence.
